About Us

History of RSM

The Rino Simoneti Memorial (R.S.M) School of Nursing was established in the year 2006 by Rt. Rev. Dr. George Mamlassery, the Bishop of Tura, Meghalaya, inaugurated by Mr. P A Sangma, Former Speaker of Lok Sabha and entrusted with the North East Society of the sisters of the Holy Cross, Menzingen, Switzerland. The Holy Cross Sisters have been engaged in health care ministry and education globally for the past 200 years. The School of Nursing is attached to the Holy Cross Hospital, Tura, established in the year 1993.

The R.S.M School of Nursing aims to prepare professionally qualified nurses to meet the health care needs of the people, in the Christian spirit of service. R.S.M School of Nursing is recognized by the Indian as well as the Meghalaya Nursing Councils for the revised General Nursing & Midwifery Course of three years. At RSM School of Nursing, we believe in the transformative power of education and the profound impact nurses make in healthcare. More than an institution, our school is a community of passionate educators, dedicated students, and visionary leaders shaping the future of healthcare.

Established with a commitment to excellence, RSM has a rich heritage of producing exceptional nursing professionals. Our graduates are not just skilled practitioners but compassionate caregivers, innovative thinkers, and advocates of health equity. Our cutting edge curriculum is designed to prepare students for the dynamic challenges of modern healthcare. Through a blend of theoretical knowledge and clinical practice, our students develop the critical thinking, clinical excellence, and ethical foundation needed to excel in their careers.

Our Founders

Fr. Theodosius Florentini

1808 - 1865

Mother Bernarda Heimgartner

1822 - 1863

We are known as ‘Sisters of the Holy Cross Menzingen.’ As women of Hope we strive to be a ‘Transforming Presence’ and bear witness to Jesus Christ in the reality where we live and work.

As Sisters of the Holy Cross we owe a great debt of gratitude to our Founder Fr. Theodosius Florentini, a Franciscan Capuchin Friar (OFM) (1808-1865) and Mother Bernarda Heimgartner our Foundress (1822-1863). Our Congregation came into existence on 16 October 1844 at Menzingen in Switzerland. The Congregation, in its readiness to be of service to the society, took up health care, education, care of orphans and women in need and various other pastoral and social works extending far and wide.

Today as promoters of life we reach out to where life is endangered. We collaborate and network with health care organizations, governments and non-governmental agencies, social leaders, church Institutions, parishes etc. for the cause where justice, mercy and equality could be upheld.
