About Us

The Rino Simoneti Memorial (R.S.M) School of Nursing was established in the year 2006 by Rt. Rev. Dr. George Mamlassery, the Bishop of Tura, Meghalaya, and entrusted with the North East Society of the sisters of the Holy Cross, Menzingen, Switzerland.

The Holy Cross Sisters have been engaged in health care ministry and education globally for the past 200 years. The School of Nursing is attached to the Holy Cross Hospital, Tura, established in the year 1993.

The R.S.M School of Nursing aims to prepare professionally qualified nurses to meet the health care needs of the people of the locality, in the Christian spirit of service.

R.S.M School of Nursing is recognized by the Indian as well as the Meghalaya Nursing Councils for the revised General Nursing & Midwifery Course of three years.

At RSM School of Nursing, we believe in the transformative power of education and the profound impact nurses make in healthcare. More than an institution, our school is a community of passionate educators, dedicated students, and visionary leaders shaping the future of healthcare.

Established with a commitment to excellence, RSM has a rich heritage of producing exceptional nursing professionals. Our graduates are not just skilled practitioners but compassionate caregivers, innovative thinkers, and advocates for health equity.

Our cutting edge curriculum is designed to prepare students for the dynamic challenges of modern healthcare. Through a blend of theoretical knowledge and hands-on clinical practice, our students develop the critical thinking, clinical skills, and ethical foundation needed to excel in their careers.
